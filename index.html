Dapper Agency — Internal

Leadership Agreements

Created together in May 2026. These agreements reflect how we want to lead — organised around the five principles that define leadership at Dapper.

01
How we deliver
Operational Excellence
Creating the conditions for high-quality output. Setting standards, removing blockers, and maintaining quality under pressure.
We give 100% to our commitments. Accountability
Not perfect — but responsible, committed, and doing our very best.
We over-communicate when things are not going to plan. Accountability
As soon as you know you can't meet a commitment, you're transparent immediately. You ask for help, and we help each other.
We do not project-manage each other's responsibilities. Accountability
You own your commitments. We don't carry each other's load. Communicate proactively when something changes.
We keep each other accountable — with clarity. Accountability
Accountability requires clarity on ownership, roles, deadlines, and how we measure success. Without clarity, accountability has no foundation.
02
How we behave
Lead by Example
Leaders set the tone through their own actions. Under pressure especially, behaviour speaks louder than words.
We stay calm, clear, and solution-focused. Under Pressure
Hard times are part of the journey. We keep our heads cool and find the most logical path forward without letting emotions take over.
We give room for emotions — and then steer towards a solution. Under Pressure
Emotions are allowed. We acknowledge them. But we take the lead in steering conversations from emotion towards what is next.
We roll up our sleeves. Under Pressure
When things get hard, we are available. We jump in where needed — not to do someone else's job, but to support.
We lead by example on feedback culture. Feedback
We actively enable juniors and mediors to give feedback directly to peers. We coach them and don't let them escalate through us.
03
How we align direction
From Vision to Practice
Translating strategy into clear priorities, decisions, and expectations — so everyone knows what they're working towards and why.
We are collaborative, not combative. Communication
We are all on the same team. Healthy friction is welcome. We don't need consensus — but we do need buy-in. Everyone gets heard, and we walk away aligned.
We provide clarity and take ownership — without taking over. Under Pressure
Under pressure we give clear priorities and expectations. We are decisive when needed. But we don't take over — others must still feel empowered to decide.
04
How we develop people
Enabling People to Grow
Leaders create the environment for growth. Feedback is the primary tool — structured, honest, and delivered without ego.
We always use the feedback framework. Feedback
Observation → feeling/effect → impact → preferred behaviour. For smaller moments, two sentences is enough — but it always needs grounding.
We give feedback directly, with context and without ego. Feedback
It is selfish not to share feedback. We give it grounded in facts, with context, adapted to the person. Praise in public; developmental feedback in private.
We receive feedback openly. Feedback
No defensiveness, no ego. We listen before judging. We share our point of view and engage in open discussion. Judgement comes after listening — not before.
05
How we interact
Direct & Open Communication
The foundation of everything. How we speak to each other determines whether trust, speed, and quality are possible at all.
We communicate directly, respectfully, and with empathy. Communication
We address each other openly and honestly, assuming positive intent. We respect each other's context, rhythm, and stage — and we never belittle one another.
We listen before we draw conclusions. Communication
We hear the other perspective before forming a judgment. We take time to properly talk and hear each other out — not rushed, not half-thought-through.
Living agreements — revisited when the team changes or when they stop holding
