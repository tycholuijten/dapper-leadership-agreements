Dapper Agency — Internal

Leadership Agreements

Created together in May 2026. These reflect how we want to work — as peers, as leaders, and as a team under pressure. They are living agreements: we revisit them when the team changes or when they stop holding.

01
How we address each other
Communication Norms
How do we want to address each other, in good times and in difficult ones?
We communicate directly, respectfully, and with empathy.
We address each other openly and honestly, assuming positive intent. We respect each other's context, rhythm, and stage — and we never belittle one another.
We listen before we draw conclusions.
We hear the other perspective before forming a judgment. We take time to properly talk and hear each other out — not rushed, not half-thought-through.
We are collaborative, not combative.
We are all on the same team. Healthy friction is welcome. We don't need consensus — but we do need buy-in. Everyone gets their say, and we walk away aligned even when we don't all agree.
You can expect from me
To be outcome-oriented in how I communicate — with clear agendas, grounded in transparency and trust, and always looking forward rather than getting stuck in a negative spiral.
02
Growth through feedback
Feedback Culture
What can you expect from me when I have something to say about your work — and what do I expect from you?
We always use the feedback framework.
Observation → feeling/effect → impact → preferred behaviour. For smaller moments this can be two sentences — but it always needs grounding.
We give feedback directly, with context and without ego.
It is selfish not to share feedback. We give it grounded in facts, not subjective emotion. We adapt our style to the person. Praise in public; share developmental feedback in private.
We receive feedback openly.
No defensiveness, no ego. We listen before judging. We share our point of view and engage in open discussion. Judgement comes after listening — not before.
We lead by example on feedback culture.
We actively enable juniors and mediors to give feedback directly to peers. We coach them. We do not let them come to us to give feedback on someone else's behalf.
You can expect from me
To give you direct, clear, constructive, and solution-minded feedback. I will share context, use proper reasoning, and help you improve — not attack you personally. And I expect you to listen, share your point of view, and engage in an open discussion.
03
Ownership & follow-through
Accountability
What can you expect from me when someone drops the ball — including when that someone is me?
We give 100% to our commitments.
When we make a commitment, we go all in. Not perfect — but responsible, committed, and doing our very best.
We over-communicate when things are not going to plan.
As soon as you know you cannot meet a commitment, you are transparent about it immediately. You don't wait until the day before. You ask for help, and we help each other.
We do not project-manage each other's responsibilities.
You own your commitments. We do not carry each other's load because someone cannot live up to agreements. Communicate proactively when something changes.
We keep each other accountable — with clarity.
Accountability requires clarity on ownership, roles, deadlines, and how we measure success. Without that clarity, accountability has no foundation.
You can expect from me
To be transparent when I drop the ball — including when that ball is mine. To ask for help and to help when someone else needs it. To always be clear about ownership and expectations.
04
When things get hard
Behaviour Under Pressure
What can you expect from me as a leader when things get hard?
We stay calm, clear, and solution-focused.
Hard times are a part of the journey. We keep our heads cool, think along, and find the most logical solution without letting emotions take over.
We give room for emotions — and then steer towards a solution.
Emotions are allowed. We acknowledge them. But we take the lead in steering conversations away from emotion and towards what is next.
We provide clarity and take ownership — without taking over.
Under pressure, we give clear priorities and clear expectations. We are decisive when needed. But we do not take over — we make sure others still feel empowered to decide.
We roll up our sleeves.
When things get hard, we are available. We jump in where needed — not to do someone else's job, but to support.
You can expect from me
To formulate and communicate a plan to get out of the situation. To stay calm and not let my stress impact the team. To give room for emotions while keeping us moving towards a solution. And to be available — with expertise, not pretension.
