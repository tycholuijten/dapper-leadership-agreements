Agreements & sub-points
01 — Operational Excellence
▲
We give 100% to our commitments. Accountability
–Not perfect — but responsible, committed, and doing our very best
–If you make a commitment, you try your very best — even if you're not able to make it, communicate on time that you need help
–Trust that your peers are doing the same
We over-communicate when things are not going to plan. Accountability
–As soon as you know you can't meet a commitment, be transparent immediately — not the day before
–Ask for help, and help each other
–I prefer over-communication if things are not going to be done according to the agreement
We do not project-manage each other's responsibilities. Accountability
–You should be able to count on your peers
–We do not carry each other's load because someone cannot live up to agreements
–Own your commitments and communicate proactively when something changes
We keep each other accountable — with clarity. Accountability
–Clarity on ownership, roles, and responsibilities
–Clarity on deadlines and KPIs — agree on them and commit
–Clarity on progress and accountability when the agreed deadline can't be met
02 — Lead by Example
▲
We stay calm, clear, and solution-focused. Under Pressure
–Stay calm under pressure — if needed, take time for yourself so the team doesn't absorb your stress
–Find the most logical solution without letting emotions get in the way
–Inspire everybody to stay positive and see the road forward
We give room for emotions — then steer towards a solution. Under Pressure
–Emotions are allowed — acknowledge them
–Take the lead in steering conversations away from emotion and towards what is next
–Do not let emotion take over the decision
We roll up our sleeves. Under Pressure
–When things get hard, be available
–Jump in where needed — not to take over, but to support
–Do the unglamorous work when needed
We lead by example on feedback culture. Feedback
–Enable juniors and mediors to give feedback quickly and openly to their teammates
–Coach the rest of the team on best practices for giving feedback
–Do not let people come to you to give feedback on someone else's behalf
–Actively ask for feedback yourself — it is selfish not to
03 — From Vision to Practice
▲
We are collaborative, not combative. Communication
–Challenge each other, even if it leads to conflict — healthy friction is welcome
–We are all on the same team working towards the same goal
–No consensus needed — but buy-in is required. Everyone gets their say; we walk away aligned
–Transparency and trust — so we avoid wasting time on politics
We provide clarity and take ownership — without taking over. Under Pressure
–Under pressure: clear priorities and clear expectations
–Be decisive when needed, but make sure others still feel empowered to make decisions
–Leadership under pressure means enabling, not controlling
–Not the best time for new suggestions — focus and clarity first
04 — Enabling People to Grow
▲
We always use the feedback framework. Feedback
–Observation → feeling/effect → impact → preferred behaviour
–Does not need to be heavy — can be two sentences for smaller moments
–For behavioural feedback especially, the full framework applies
We give feedback directly, with context and without ego. Feedback
–It is selfish not to share feedback — say it
–Direct, clear, constructive, and solution-minded
–Grounded in facts — not subjective emotion or personal criticism
–Share context; adjust communication style to the person in front of you
–Praise in public; share developmental feedback in private
We receive feedback openly. Feedback
–No defensiveness, no ego
–Listen before judging — judgement comes after listening, not before
–Share your point of view and have an open discussion
–Be open to being proven wrong
05 — Direct & Open Communication
▲
We communicate directly, respectfully, and with empathy. Communication
–Assume positive intent — do not make assumptions
–Low hierarchy / equal communication — never belittle someone else
–Respect each other's rhythm, context, and stage
–Be vulnerable — acknowledge when you don't know something
We listen before we draw conclusions. Communication
–Hear the other perspective before forming a judgment
–Take time to properly talk and hear each other out — not rushed, not half-thought-through
–Put yourself in the shoes of the other — especially when someone is stressed
We are outcome-oriented in how we communicate. Communication
–Outcome-oriented calls with clear agendas
–Solution-oriented — don't get stuck in a negative spiral
–Grounded in transparency and trust — always looking forward
Living agreements — revisited when the team changes or when they stop holding
